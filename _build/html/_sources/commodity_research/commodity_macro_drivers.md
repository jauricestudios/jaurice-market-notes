# Global Macro Drivers of Commodity Markets

Commodity markets sit at the intersection of the real economy and financial systems.

Prices are influenced not only by physical supply and demand conditions but also by broader macroeconomic forces.

Understanding commodity markets therefore requires analysing both global macroeconomic dynamics and the structure of physical supply chains.

## Global Macroeconomic Drivers

Several macroeconomic variables influence commodity prices.

### Global Economic Growth

Industrial commodities such as copper, iron ore, and oil are closely linked to global economic activity.

Manufacturing output, infrastructure investment, and industrial production all influence demand for raw materials.

### Interest Rates and Monetary Policy

Interest rates influence commodity markets through the cost of capital, inventory financing, and investment flows.

Higher interest rates can reduce commodity demand by slowing economic growth.

### Currency Markets

Most globally traded commodities are priced in U.S. dollars.

Changes in the value of the dollar therefore influence commodity prices worldwide.

### Inflation Dynamics

Energy and food prices play a significant role in global inflation cycles and often serve as indicators of broader economic conditions.

## Physical Commodity Market Drivers

Commodity prices are also strongly influenced by physical market structures.

### Global Supply Flows

Production from major exporting regions determines global supply conditions.

Examples include oil production from OPEC countries, copper output from Chile, and grain harvests from major agricultural regions.

### Shipping Routes and Strategic Chokepoints

Many commodities move through key maritime routes such as:

- Strait of Hormuz
- Suez Canal
- Strait of Malacca

Disruptions to these routes can significantly affect global supply.

### Inventory Levels

Inventory levels provide an important measure of market tightness.

Low inventories often signal supply shortages, while high inventories may indicate oversupply.

### Futures Curve Structure

Commodity futures markets reflect expectations about supply and demand conditions.

Markets may move into:

- backwardation when near-term supply is tight
- contango when inventories are abundant

### Geopolitical Risk

Political instability, sanctions, trade disputes, and conflict can all influence commodity production and transportation.

### Weather and Climate

Weather patterns strongly affect agricultural production and can also influence energy demand.

Understanding how macroeconomic forces interact with physical supply chains is central to analysing commodity markets.
