# Monte Carlo Methods in Financial Modelling

Monte Carlo simulation is one of the most widely used numerical methods in modern quantitative finance.

The method approximates complex expectations by simulating large numbers of random outcomes and estimating their average behaviour.

This approach is particularly useful when pricing derivatives whose payoff depends on the entire path of an asset price rather than only its final value.

## Discovering Monte Carlo Methods

I first encountered Monte Carlo simulation during my third year while studying derivatives pricing and stochastic modelling.

What initially appeared to be a simple computational method quickly revealed itself to be one of the most powerful tools in quantitative finance.

Instead of solving complex analytical equations, Monte Carlo methods allow researchers to simulate thousands or millions of potential market paths and estimate expected outcomes directly.

This insight demonstrated how numerical simulation can be used to solve financial problems that would otherwise be mathematically intractable.

## Applications in Finance

Monte Carlo simulation is widely used across financial markets, including:

- pricing exotic derivatives
- portfolio risk analysis
- value-at-risk estimation
- scenario analysis and stress testing
- pricing path-dependent securities

Because many financial derivatives depend on the evolution of asset prices over time, simulation methods provide a flexible framework for analysing complex financial instruments.

## Connection to Dissertation Research

Monte Carlo simulation forms the core methodology of my dissertation research, which focuses on pricing arithmetic Asian options under a Geometric Brownian Motion framework.

The research investigates convergence behaviour, discretisation effects, and techniques for improving the efficiency of simulation-based pricing models.
