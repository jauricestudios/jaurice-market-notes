# Supply Monitor

This page tracks the supply side of the UK Gas & LNG Market Monitor.

The purpose is to understand whether the UK gas system is receiving enough gas from domestic production, Norway, LNG imports, storage withdrawals and interconnector flows.

## Core question

Is UK gas supply becoming tighter or looser, and which supply channel is driving the change?

## Main supply channels

The UK gas system is supplied through several physical channels.

| Supply channel | Meaning | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| UK continental shelf production | Domestic gas production from the North Sea | Local supply reduces import dependence |
| Norwegian pipeline flows | Gas imported from Norway through pipelines | Norway is one of the most important UK supply sources |
| LNG sendout | Regasified LNG entering the grid from terminals | Flexible but globally competitive supply |
| Storage withdrawals | Gas withdrawn from storage sites | Important in winter or during supply stress |
| Interconnector imports | Gas entering Britain from Europe or Ireland | Links UK tightness to European conditions |

## Why LNG matters

LNG is flexible because cargoes can move globally. However, that flexibility also means the UK competes with Europe and Asia for cargoes.

If Asian demand rises, or if shipping routes become riskier, LNG may become more expensive or less available to Europe. This can feed into UK NBP prices and wider power-market costs.

## Why Norway matters

Norwegian pipeline flows are central to UK supply. Outages, maintenance or reduced flows can tighten the market quickly, especially during high-demand periods.

For a UK gas analyst, Norwegian flows are one of the first things to monitor.

## Why storage matters

Storage does not create new supply, but it changes timing. High storage gives the system flexibility. Low storage increases winter vulnerability.

Storage is especially important when:

- demand is high,
- wind generation is low,
- LNG arrivals are weak,
- pipeline supply is disrupted,
- European gas balances are tight.

## Supply tightness logic

A simple supply stress score can be built from:

| Indicator | Tightness signal |
|---|---|
| Falling domestic production | Tighter |
| Lower Norwegian flows | Tighter |
| Lower LNG sendout | Tighter |
| Storage withdrawals rising sharply | Tighter |
| Interconnector exports rising | Tighter for UK |
| High LNG share of supply | More exposed to global LNG competition |

3. Did the supply mix make the UK system tighter or looser?
4. What should be watched next week?
