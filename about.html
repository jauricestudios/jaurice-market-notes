About#

I am building a research portfolio at the intersection of quantitative # About

This research notebook documents my work studying quantitative finance, derivatives pricing, and global commodity markets.

My academic background is in mathematics and financial modelling, with a particular focus on stochastic processes, simulation methods, and the mathematical foundations of modern derivatives markets.

Alongside academic work, I am developing a structured research framework exploring how global macroeconomic dynamics, financial derivatives, and physical commodity markets interact.

The aim of this project is to build a research base that bridges theoretical finance, applied quantitative modelling, and real-world market analysis.

Current Areas of Study#

My current research focuses on:

  • stochastic calculus and continuous-time finance

  • Monte Carlo simulation methods

  • derivatives pricing and exotic options

  • global macroeconomic drivers of markets

  • commodity market supply chains and trade flows

  • financial risk modelling and market structure

Books Currently Studying#

Quantitative Finance#

  • Steven Shreve — Stochastic Calculus for Finance II

  • John Hull — Options, Futures and Other Derivatives

  • Paul Glasserman — Monte Carlo Methods in Financial Engineering

Commodity Markets & Energy Trading#

  • Iris Mack — Energy Trading and Risk Management

  • M.A.H. Dempster & Ke Tang — Commodities: Futures, Forwards and Derivatives Pricing

These texts provide the theoretical and practical foundations for the research projects documented throughout this notebook.

Research Philosophy#

My approach combines three perspectives:

  1. Mathematical finance — stochastic modelling, derivatives pricing, and simulation methods.

  2. Global macroeconomics — understanding how economic cycles, interest rates, and currencies influence markets.

  3. Physical commodity systems — analysing how supply chains, logistics, and geopolitical risks shape commodity prices.

Understanding how these systems interact is essential for analysing modern financial markets.
