# Research Projects

This section documents independent research projects analysing the interaction between global macroeconomics, commodity markets, and financial derivatives.

## Oil Inventory Dynamics and Futures Curve Structure

This project investigates how global oil inventory levels influence the structure of the WTI crude oil futures curve.

Changes in inventory levels affect storage economics and market expectations, which can lead to shifts between contango and backwardation in futures markets.

The analysis combines data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) with economic theory including storage theory and convenience yield models.

## Global Wheat Supply Shocks and Price Volatility

Agricultural commodity markets are highly sensitive to weather conditions and production shocks.

This project analyses how global wheat production shocks influence futures price volatility and market expectations.

Key drivers include:

- crop yields
- weather patterns
- global grain inventories
- export restrictions and trade policies

## Commodity Market Risk Dashboard (In Development)

A longer-term project involves developing a commodity risk monitoring framework that tracks signals from multiple sources, including:

- geopolitical developments
- shipping flows
- inventory changes
- futures spreads
- macroeconomic indicators

The objective is to build a structured system for monitoring emerging risks across global commodity markets.
