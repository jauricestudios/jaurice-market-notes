Jaurice O’Connor#

I am a final-year Mathematics student building practical research tools around energy markets, commodity risk, infrastructure exposure and applied modelling.

My work combines Python, Excel, GIS and economic reasoning to turn physical systems into clearer market and risk signals.

My dissertation focused on Monte Carlo methods for financial modelling, giving me experience with Python, simulation, uncertainty analysis and model evaluation. I am now applying these skills to real-world problems in commodities, infrastructure and economic research.

Current focus#

I am currently building a public research portfolio around energy markets, commodity risk, infrastructure exposure and applied economic modelling.

The main idea is simple: use mathematics, data and market reasoning to understand how physical systems become financial or commercial risks.

At the moment, my active focus is the GB Energy Tightness Monitor, which looks at how UK power, gas, LNG, storage and interconnector conditions can affect market pressure.

Flagship project#

GB Energy Tightness Monitor#

Status: Active prototype
Theme: UK power, gas, LNG, storage and interconnector pressure
Core question: Is the GB energy system becoming tighter or looser, and which physical drivers explain it?

This is my main active build. It combines commodity-market research with Python dashboards, GIS mapping, price stress scales, flow diagrams and analyst-style commentary.

The purpose is not just to display prices. The aim is to connect physical energy-market drivers to commercial interpretation: demand, wind output, gas burn, LNG sendout, storage, interconnectors, TTF, JKM and wider commodity risk.

Open standalone dashboard
View project page

What it demonstrates: Python, Plotly, GIS/Folium, UK gas and power-market research, commodity-price interpretation, physical-flow thinking and dashboard design.

Next step: Replace manual prototype values with real Elexon/BMRS generation data, National Gas flows, LNG sendout and European storage data.

Featured Projects#

These projects are designed to show how I use mathematics, Python, GIS, economic research and market reasoning to understand commodity, infrastructure and risk problems.

1. GB Energy Tightness Monitor#

Status: Active prototype
Theme: UK power, gas, LNG, storage and interconnector pressure
Purpose: Understand whether the GB energy system is becoming tighter or looser, and which physical drivers explain it.

This is my main active build. It combines commodity-market research with Python dashboards, GIS mapping, stress scales, flow diagrams and analyst-style commentary.

Open standalone dashboard
View project page

2. Climate Infrastructure Exposure Map#

Status: Prototype
Theme: Physical climate risk, infrastructure exposure and insurance-style analytics
Purpose: Identify which UK energy and transport assets may warrant closer physical climate-risk review.

This project uses geospatial analysis to connect asset locations, regional geography and exposure screening. It is useful for infrastructure risk, insurance analytics, climate adaptation and asset-level risk assessment.

What it demonstrates: Python, GeoPandas, spatial joins, exposure scoring, regional summaries and infrastructure-risk communication.

3. Copper Demand Scenario Model#

Status: Planned build
Theme: Electrification, AI infrastructure and commodity demand
Purpose: Model how grid expansion, data-centre growth and electrification could affect medium-term copper demand.

This project will sit at the intersection of economic research, energy-transition analysis and commodity demand modelling.

What it will demonstrate: Python, scenario modelling, sensitivity analysis, economic research and commodity-market reasoning.

4. Co-location Commercial Valuation Model#

Status: Planned build
Theme: Infrastructure economics and commercial valuation
Purpose: Compare when co-located infrastructure assets may create more value than standalone assets.

This project will focus on commercial logic, pricing assumptions, utilisation, cost sensitivity and scenario-based valuation.

What it will demonstrate: Excel, Python, pricing logic, sensitivity analysis and commercial risk thinking.

5. Geopolitical Energy Risk Monitor#

Status: Planned build
Theme: Chokepoints, sanctions, war risk, shipping disruption and commodity exposure
Purpose: Track how geopolitical events can affect energy and commodity supply chains.

This project will eventually connect maps, event risk, sanctions monitoring and commodity-flow exposure.

What it will demonstrate: geopolitical risk framing, commodity-market research, GIS, dashboards and policy-style analysis.
