## Featured Projects

These projects are designed to show how I use mathematics, Python, GIS, economic research and market reasoning to understand commodity, infrastructure and risk problems.

They are not just coding exercises. The aim is to build analyst-style tools that connect data, physical systems and commercial interpretation.

## 1. GB Energy Tightness Monitor

**Status:** Active prototype  
**Theme:** UK power, gas, LNG, storage and interconnector pressure  
**Purpose:** Understand whether the GB energy system is becoming tighter or looser, and which physical drivers explain it.

This is my main active build. It combines commodity-market research with Python dashboards, GIS mapping, price stress scales, flow diagrams and analyst-style commentary.

[Open standalone dashboard](_static/gb_energy_monitor/dashboard.html)  
[View project page](projects/gb-energy-monitor/index.md)

**What it demonstrates:** Python, Plotly, GIS/Folium, UK gas and power-market research, commodity-price interpretation, physical-flow thinking and dashboard design.

**Next step:** Replace manual values with real Elexon/BMRS generation data, National Gas flows, LNG sendout and European storage data.

## 2. Climate Infrastructure Exposure Map

**Status:** Prototype  
**Theme:** Physical climate risk, infrastructure exposure and insurance-style analytics  
**Purpose:** Identify which UK energy and transport assets may warrant closer physical climate-risk review.

This project uses geospatial analysis to connect asset locations, regional geography and exposure screening. It is useful for infrastructure risk, insurance analytics, climate adaptation and asset-level risk assessment.

**What it demonstrates:** Python, GeoPandas, spatial joins, exposure scoring, regional summaries and infrastructure-risk communication.

## 5. Geopolitical Energy Risk Monitor
